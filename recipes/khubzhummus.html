Khubz with Hummus



Description

Hummus with khubz is a classic Middle Eastern combination enjoyed as a snack, appetizer, or light meal. Creamy hummus, made from blended chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and garlic, is paired with warm, soft khubz (Arabic pita bread) for dipping and scooping. This simple yet flavorful dish is rich in protein, fiber, and healthy fats, making it both nutritious and satisfying.

Ingredients

For the Hummus

  • 1 can (400g) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 3 tablespoons tahini
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1/2 cloves garlic
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2/4 tablespoons cold water
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • Paprika and extra olive oil for garnish (optional)

For the Khubz

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 teaspoons instant yeast
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Steps

Prepare the Khubz

  1. In a bowl, combine warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for 5 minutes until foamy.
  2. Add the flour, salt, and olive oil. Mix until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Cover and let rise for 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  5. Divide the dough into 6 equal portions and roll each into a thin circle.
  6. Preheat the oven to 230°C (450°F) with a baking tray inside.
  7. Bake each pita for 3–5 minutes until puffed and lightly golden.
  8. Wrap the baked bread in a clean towel to keep it soft.

Prepare the Hummus

  1. Add the chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, garlic, olive oil, and salt to a food processor.
  2. Blend until smooth.
  3. Gradually add cold water until the hummus reaches a creamy consistency.
  4. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.
  5. Transfer to a serving bowl and garnish with olive oil and paprika.
Prep Time: 30 minutes
Rise Time: 1 hour
Cook Time: 5 minutes
Servings: 4-6
